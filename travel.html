Get to New York City

BY PLANE


Newark Airport: Conveniently located about an hour outside of the city. Taxi fares range from $60–$80. We recommend taking the AirTrain to the NJ Transit Terminal, and hopping aboard a train that goes directly to Penn Station for $18.50.

JFK Airport: A taxi will take about 45 minutes (depending on traffic) and costs $65. We recommend taking the AirTrain to Jamaica station, and catching an LIRR train directly to Penn Station for $7.25 (though you can also take the A line of the subway for $2.50).

LaGuardia Airport: By far the most unpleasant option, VP Joe Biden has likened this airport to a third world country. There are no reasonable public transport options to downtown Manhattan; a cab takes 20 minutes if you're lucky (90 minutes if you're very unlucky), and prices start around $40. We can't in good conscience recommend LaGuardia.

BY TRAIN


Penn Station: Located in the heart of Manhattan and the pits of Hell, Penn Station is both tremendously convenient and tremendously unpleasant. All Manhattan-bound NJT and LIRR trains end here. We recommend taking a train in–and then getting out of Herald Square as soon as possible.

Grand Central: This iconic train station is located just blocks from Times Square, and is unfortunately only serviced by Metro North. If you're coming from upstate New York or Connecticut, this is a great option for you. Otherwise, it makes for a fun tourist stop (and yes, there's a Shake Shack).

BY AUTOMOBILE


Lincoln Tunnel: The Lincoln Tunnel connects New Jersey to midtown Manhattan through NJ Route 495. The highway will drive you right past the Weehawken Dueling Grounds (RIP Hamilton) and onto West 39th Street in Manhattan.

Holland Tunnel: The Holland Tunnel connects New Jersey to lower Manhattan through I-78 and NJ-139. The Holland Tunnel dumps you out right on Canal Street, so you can reward yourself with soup dumplings after a long drive.

George Washington Bridge: Named after our first president, the GW Bridge connects northern NJ to uptown Manhattan (In the Heights!) through I-95 and the NJ Turnpike.
